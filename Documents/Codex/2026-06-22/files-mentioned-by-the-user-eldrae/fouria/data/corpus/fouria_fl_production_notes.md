# FOURIA FL Studio Production Notes

These are original starter notes for FOURIA's local RAG memory. Add your own
beat breakdowns, MIDI exports, channel presets, prompt/response pairs, and FL
workflows here over time.

## Fast FL Studio Loop Workflow

1. Set tempo and key before sound selection.
2. Build an 8-bar loop with chords, melody, drums, 808, and one ear-candy sound.
3. Route every major sound to the mixer immediately: drums, 808, melody, counter,
   vocals, and effects.
4. Gain-stage before mixing. Leave the master below clipping and keep the 808
   controlled before adding loudness.
5. Duplicate the loop into arrangement: intro, hook, verse, hook, bridge or break,
   final hook, outro.

## Piano Roll

Use scale highlighting to stay in key, then break the grid only after the idea is
clear. Use Alt+R for velocity variation and Alt+S for strum. Keep low notes away
from the 808 unless they intentionally support the root.

## Chords

Dark trap in minor keys often works with i-VI-III-VII, i-iv-VI-v, i-VII-VI-VII,
and i-III-VII-VI. For F minor, useful chords include Fm, Db, Ab, Eb, Bbm, and Cm.
Keep chord voicings simple, then add tension with top-note movement, octave
doubles, passing notes, and sparse counter melodies.

## Melody

Strong melodies use repetition plus one small change. Start with the root, minor
third, fifth, sixth, or seventh. Use call-and-response between the main melody and
counter melody. Avoid filling every beat; leave space for drums and vocals.

## Drums

Kick and clap/snare define the bounce. Closed hats can carry motion, but velocity
and rests matter more than constant rolls. Add open hats and percs at phrase ends
or before drops. Make the first drum pass simple enough that the 808 pattern can
breathe.

## 808

Tune the 808 to the song key and make it monophonic unless glide is intentional.
Follow chord roots first, then add passing notes. If the low end is muddy, shorten
808 notes, remove low melody notes, reduce overlap with kick, and use saturation
instead of just turning the 808 up.

## Mixing

Clean production starts with levels. EQ is for removing conflicts before boosting
character. High-pass non-bass instruments when needed, but do not thin everything
out automatically. Use reference tracks for balance, not for copying every sound.

## Exporting For Critique

Export MIDI for notes, timing, and harmony critique. Export stems named by role:
kick, 808, snare, hats, melody, counter, vocal, fx. Include BPM and key in the
folder name when possible.
