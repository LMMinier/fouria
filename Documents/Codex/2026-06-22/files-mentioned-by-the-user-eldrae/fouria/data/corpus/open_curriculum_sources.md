# FOURIA Open Production Curriculum Sources

This file is a source registry. FOURIA uses distilled notes and retains
attribution. It does not claim that open educational material alone verifies an
automated FL Studio action.

## Mixing and Mastering — Wikibooks

- Source: https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Mixing_and_Mastering
- License: CC BY-SA (Wikibooks)
- Curriculum: source selection, balance, bass mixdown, dynamics, spatial
  processing, monitoring, and mastering workflow.
- Core lesson: solve arrangement and source problems before reaching for EQ;
  low-end clarity begins with compatible sounds, register, and timing.

## Sound Synthesis Theory — Wikibooks

- Source: https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Sound_Synthesis_Theory
- License: CC BY-SA (Wikibooks)
- Curriculum: oscillators, spectra, envelopes, modulation, subtractive and
  additive concepts, and timbral design.
- Application: translate synthesis concepts into FL generators and third-party
  plugins without tying knowledge to a single interface.

## Sound Recording — Wikibooks

- Source: https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Sound_Recording
- License: CC BY-SA (Wikibooks)
- Curriculum: signal flow, recording levels, microphones, monitoring, and
  editing fundamentals.

## Open Music Theory

- Source: https://viva.pressbooks.pub/openmusictheory/
- Catalog: https://open.umn.edu/opentextbooks/textbooks/1254
- License: open educational resource; verify chapter attribution when importing.
- Curriculum: fundamentals, rhythm, meter, diatonic/chromatic harmony, form,
  counterpoint, jazz, popular music, and orchestration.
- Application: chord function, voice leading, phrase design, harmonic rhythm,
  tension/release, formal contrast, and genre-aware composition.

## Learning policy

1. Store source URL and license with every imported lesson.
2. Prefer distilled operational rules and exercises over copied prose.
3. Separate universal audio principles from genre taste.
4. Treat loudness, EQ frequencies, compressor settings, and plugin chains as
   context-dependent starting points—not laws.
5. Mark FL actions verified only after observable project-state evidence.
