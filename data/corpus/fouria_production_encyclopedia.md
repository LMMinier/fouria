# FOURIA Production Knowledge Core

## Production method
Clarify or infer genre, subgenre, BPM, key, scale, mood, section length, and
requested instrument. Build in layers: harmonic foundation, main motif, rhythm
section, bass/808, counterpoint, transitions, arrangement, mix, then master.
Every layer needs a role. Remove parts that compete for rhythm, register, or
attention. Save headroom and verify actions inside FL Studio.

## FL Studio workflow
Use Channel Rack for generators and patterns, Piano Roll for note programming,
Playlist for arrangement, Mixer for routing and processing, Browser for assets,
automation clips for movement, Patcher for reusable chains, Edison for editing,
NewTone for pitch/time work, and reference tracks routed separately from the
master processing chain. Name, color, route, gain-stage, and organize channels.
Use ghost notes, scale highlighting, note properties, strum, arpeggiation,
velocity, swing, and controlled timing variation deliberately.

## Harmony and melody
Choose a scale appropriate to the mood. Establish chord function, voice-lead
between nearby notes, avoid unnecessary low-register chord mud, and reserve
space for bass. Strong melodies combine motif repetition, rhythmic identity,
contour, rests, tension notes, and phrase-end resolution. Countermelodies answer
rather than duplicate the lead.

## Drums and bass
Kick and bass must cooperate in pitch, timing, envelope, and frequency. Tune
808s, control note overlap, use slides intentionally, and write bass rhythm
around the kick. Groove comes from placement, velocity, sound length, swing,
syncopation, silence, and call-and-response—not random complexity.

## Arrangement
Create contrast through subtraction, register, density, automation, fills,
transitions, harmonic change, and new countermelodies. Typical sections include
intro, verse/build, hook/drop, variation, bridge/breakdown, final hook/drop, and
outro, but genre and song intent decide the form.

## Mixing
Start with balance and gain staging. Correct source and arrangement problems
before processing. Use EQ for conflicts, compression for dynamics/envelope,
saturation for density, transient shaping for attack/sustain, reverb and delay
for depth, and automation for musical movement. Check mono, low volume,
headphones, speakers, spectrum, phase, clipping, and reference translation.

## Mastering
Mastering cannot repair a broken mix. Preserve transients and tonal balance,
control peaks, make small broad corrections, compare loudness-matched
references, and choose final loudness for the genre and delivery platform.
Avoid chasing loudness at the expense of punch, depth, or distortion.

## Genre grammar
- Trap: 120–160 BPM commonly felt half-time; sparse minor harmony, agile hats,
  hard snare/clap backbeat, tuned 808s, negative space, short motifs.
- Drill: sliding bass, dark modes, syncopated kick, sparse snare placement,
  tense bells/strings/pianos, abrupt rhythmic gaps.
- Boom bap: sampled or sample-like harmony, swung drums, strong snare, bass
  pocket, chops, texture, and human timing.
- R&B/neo-soul: extended chords, voice leading, pocket, warm bass, expressive
  melody, restrained drums, tasteful chromatic approach notes.
- Pop: immediate hook, clear structure, controlled density, strong transitions,
  layered vocals/melodies, polished low end and top end.
- House/techno: four-on-floor foundation, bass-kick relationship, evolving
  automation, loop variation, tension/release, DJ-friendly phrasing.
- Amapiano: log-drum conversation, deep groove, shakers/percussion, spacious
  harmony, long-form development, restrained melodic hooks.
- Afrobeats/dancehall/reggaeton/dembow: interlocking percussion, syncopated
  bass, concise hooks, rhythmic guitars/keys, vocal space, genre-specific clave
  and kick/snare relationships.
- DnB/jungle: break manipulation, fast tempo, sub control, syncopation, pads or
  stabs, energetic phrase edits.
- Cinematic/orchestral: motif, orchestration by register and function, dynamics,
  articulation, harmonic pacing, and narrative development.

## Command policy
For “make/build/add” requests, produce an executable plan and perform only
supported verified actions. Report generated key, scale, BPM, notes, channels,
plugins, mixer routing, and files. If FL automation is uncertain, say where it
stopped instead of claiming success.
